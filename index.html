Upcoming Events

Throughout the Solar System and Beyond

Thursday, January 10, from 8:00am to 10:00am

Travel through the vast expanse of space with Chris Highlen, Observatory Director for the Fort Wayne Astronomical Society, as he takes us on a journey of our galaxy via PowerPoint. Learn the latest scientific facts about our sister planets and then go beyond to other galaxies, nebulas, and stars.

Nature Photography: How to Take That Great Nature Picture

Wednesday, January 16, from 7:00pm to 8:00pm

Ever wonder why what you saw in your camera's viewfinder isn't the same as what you find later on its memory card or the print? Expert photographer Paul McAfee will share some of the do's, don'ts, and tricks of taking nature photographs to help you improve the quality of your nature pictures. Meet at the Aboite Branch Public Library, 5630 Coventry Lane, Fort Wayne.

Signs, Tracks, and Scat II

Saturday, February 2, from 9:00am to 10:30am

Dane Nagy, Indiana Master Naturalist and tracker, will conduct a second session on how to become more aware of the many animals around us through signs, tracks, and scat. We will again start in the Eagle Marsh barn using the tracking boxes to create some of our own tracks, hone our skills on how to interpret what we see and then go outside to observe what the animals of Eagle Marsh have left behind for us in the snow and mud. Come even if you didn't attend the first session as Dane will be introducing new concepts and skills. Dress warmly!

What's Happening at Little River Wetlands Project in 2013?

Thursday, February 14, from 8:00am to 10:00am

Be the first to hear about planned projects and changes at Eagle Marsh, Arrowhead Prairie, and Little River Landing in 2013 as Betsy Yankowiak, LRWP's Director of Preserves and Programs, highlights via Powerpoint and through discussion where LRWP has been and what we hope to accomplish in the new year at your favorite preserves

Let's Hear it for Salamanders!

Thursday, February 21, from 7:00pm to 8:00pm

Join Ryan Hunt, graduate student and researcher with the IPFW Biology department, to learn the results of his extensive study of the salamanders of Eagle Marsh via Powerpoint and discussion. Our wetlands are vital to this timid, egg-laying amphibian whose hidden life is far more interesting than you might expect. And salamanders also impact us. Meet at the Fox Island County Park Nature Center, 7324 Yohne Road, Fort Wayne.
